# Notes — addepar-investment-memos-and-decision-making

Source selected (text-native preferred): `research/sources/addepar-investment-memos-and-decision-making.md`

## Key ideas
- Investment memos are core to investment decision quality and process transparency.
- Most professional investors use formal memo templates/processes.
- High-value memo process includes: screening, writing, critique, rating, approval, storage, and learning.
- Memos should cover opportunity, portfolio fit, and due-diligence process.
- Memo quality is affected by process friction and author incentives/conflicts.

## Claims
- 97% of surveyed investors reported a formal memo template/process.
- 78% reported requiring memo approval before making an investment.
- Typical memo sections include deal description, risks, team, and terms.
- Memos can improve investment outcomes by improving decision processes.

## Evidence
- Source references a June 2022 survey (54 organizations) plus six October 2022 case studies.
- Document notes no peer-reviewed literature on investment memos as of March 2023.

## Definitions
- Investment memo: structured artifact documenting logic, assumptions, risks, terms, and decision context.
- Investment logic: rationale for why an opportunity fits strategy and portfolio.
- Portfolio fit: expected role in diversification/exposure objectives.
